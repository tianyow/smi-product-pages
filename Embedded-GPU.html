          Overview

          Silicon Motion provides cutting-edge embedded graphics solutions tailored for a wide array of applications, including industrial PCs, servers, high-resolution handheld devices, factory human-machine interfaces, point-of-sale terminals, medical equipment, and casino gaming machines. Our embedded GPU products are thoughtfully packaged within multi-chip modules (MCMs) featuring embedded video memory, offering an optimal balance between cost efficiency, power consumption reduction, and device longevity. The integration of fundamental graphics capabilities empowers our products with exceptional reliability and adaptability to cater to the diverse needs of various industries.

          The Silicon Motion Graphic Display SoCs have been meticulously crafted to cater to diverse application requirements.

          Graphics Software FAQ

          Find quick answers to your Graphics product questions!

          The SMI Graphics Software FAQ mainly provides answers to common questions.

          The driver of Silicon Motion graphics software generates a "virtual desktop" image, which is sent through a USB connection to the adapter. The pixels of this image are then rendered by the Silicon Motion chip integrated into the adapter.

          Due to the significant amount of data being sent by the software driver, the data had to be compressed to efficiently pass through USB connections. This is why some images may not appear as "crisp" when using the Silicon Motion graphics adapter or docking station.

          No, the driver of Silicon Motion graphics software does not support blue light reduction, so applications such as f.lux, Night shift won't affect the display.

          No, the Silicon Motion graphics adapter or docking station does not support OpenGL. It operates as a virtual graphics adapter, relying on host laptop's CPU and GPU to generate the image.
